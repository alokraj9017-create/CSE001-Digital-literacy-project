### 🔍 Case Study: The "OLX QR Code" Phishing Scam
**Type of Crime:** Financial Fraud / Phishing

**Description:** A common cybercrime in India involves fraudsters posing as buyers on second-hand marketplaces like OLX. The attacker contacts a seller and agrees to the price immediately to build trust. Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a **QR Code** to the seller via WhatsApp, claiming that scanning it will "authorize" the payment into the seller's account. 

**The Hook:** The victim, expecting to receive money, scans the code and enters their UPI PIN. In reality, the QR code is a "Request Money" link. By entering their PIN, the victim authorizes a deduction rather than a deposit.

**Impact:** Victims often lose thousands of rupees instantly. Because the victim "authorized" the transaction with their PIN, recovering the funds from banks is extremely difficult.

---

### 💳 UPI & Digital Payment Safety Checklist
To prevent falling victim to such scams, follow these 5 essential rules:

1.  **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** You **never** need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. If someone asks you to do this, it is a scam.
2.  **Verify the Identity:** Always double-check the name of the receiver appearing on the UPI app before hitting 'Pay'.
3.  **Use Official Apps Only:** Never click on payment links sent via SMS or WhatsApp. Always open your official banking or UPI app (BHIM, GPay, PhonePe) directly.
4.  **Set Transaction Limits:** Limit the maximum daily amount for UPI transactions in your bank settings to minimize potential loss.
5.  **Report Immediately:** If a fraud occurs, call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at **1930** or report it at `cybercrime.gov.in` within the first "Golden Hour."
