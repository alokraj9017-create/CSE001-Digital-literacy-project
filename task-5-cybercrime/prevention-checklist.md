# 🛡️ Digital Safety & UPI Prevention Checklist
**Student Digital Ambassador Program | Task 5**

As digital transactions become the norm, staying alert to "Social Engineering" tactics is the best defense against cybercrime. Use this checklist to stay secure.

---

### 💳 UPI & Financial Safety (The 5 Golden Rules)
1. **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** You never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. If someone asks you to do this, it is a 100% scam.
2. **Verify the "Display Name":** Before hitting 'Pay' or entering your PIN, always look at the name that appears on the UPI app to ensure it matches the person or store you intend to pay.
3. **Avoid Third-Party Screen Sharing:** Never download apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer if requested by a "customer care" representative over the phone; they use these to see your OTPs and PINs.
4. **Limit Transaction Amounts:** Use your bank's mobile app to set a daily limit for UPI transactions. This minimizes the "damage" if your account is ever compromised.
5. **The "Golden Hour" Rule:** If you are defrauded, report it within 1 hour to the National Cyber Crime Helpline (**1930**) or `cybercrime.gov.in` to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds.

---

### 🌐 General Cyber Hygiene
* **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Enable 2FA on your college email, WhatsApp, and Instagram accounts immediately.
* **Public Wi-Fi Warning:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or making payments while connected to free public Wi-Fi (like at airports or cafes) without a VPN.
* **Link Skepticism:** Do not click on "shortened links" (bit.ly/...) sent via SMS claiming you have won a prize or that your electricity/SIM card will be disconnected.
